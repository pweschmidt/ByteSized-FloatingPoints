# ByteSized-FloatingPoints

## What is this ByteSized course about?
Floating point numbers are an essential type of data in computers. They are used to represent e.g. real numbers (e.g. 2.5 of the value of "pi").
But representing real numbers in bits has some important limitations. 
In this course we look at some of these limitations and how they can lead to (at times unexpected) results. 


## ByteSized dRTP
ByteSized dRTP (digital research technical professional) is a training programme run by the STEP-UP project in the UK. It consists of an online course followed by a companion podcast episode on [Code for Thought](https://codeforthought.buzzsprout.com). 

The online courses are short sessions (1- 1 1/2 hours) and focus on a specific skill or theme that might be relevant in your work as a digital research technical professional - be that a research software engineer, a data manager, a librarian or other technician. 

Topics range from technical skills, e.g. the use of ***git*** to questions around project management and development practices. 

The [STEP-UP](https://step-up.ac.uk/events/bytesized-drtp/) project is funded by UKRI and is run by Imperial College London, University College London, King's College London and the University of Westminster.
